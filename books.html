Quest Packs & Adventures

Here’s the deal: I write about serious stuff; philosophy, society, the human condition, why bananas are curved,
but I wrap it in stories that involve a sarcastic hoopoe, a snack‑obsessed student named Jimba, his classmates,
and a classroom that accidentally travels through time and space.

Some of these dives go very deep. Like, past the bottom of the rabbit hole.
You might resurface questioning your existence, your grocery list, and whether Die Hard is a Christmas movie (it is).

That’s normal. Or not. I’m not a therapist.

What’s inside an Adventure / Quest Pack?
#A full story (no abridgement; you get the whole adventure)
#An Imaginarium Kit (simple materials to build, taste, or draw)
#Bennu’s Field Log (portfolio prompts; draw, write, reflect)
#Cross‑curricular connections for the nerds
#A “Ibn Firnas Flight Festival” corner to celebrate glorious failures

"Animal Testing" – a short story. Download PDF

Aisha hid the rabbit in the art supply closet before class. She knew she couldn’t keep it secret forever. Ms. Magda wanted data, skin reaction, redness, swelling. “It’s one small animal,” the teacher said. “For science. For human good.” But Aisha looked at the rabbit’s nose, soft and twitching. "Cultural Appropriation" – a short story. Download PDF

Leila watched Fatima put on the dirac – the Somali dress her own grandmother had sewn. Fatima called it “exotic.” She would wear it for a fashion show, then return to her own clothes. Leila’s grandmother stood at the back, silent. Afterward, Leila took Fatima aside. “Would you wear my skin, too?” she asked. Fatima blinked. The question hung like a dress on a hook.
